Practice MCQ Questions

1. What is the SI unit of force?

  • A) Joule
  • B) Newton
  • C) Watt
  • D) Pascal
Correct Answer: B) Newton
Force is measured in Newtons (N) in the SI system. One Newton is defined as the force required to accelerate a mass of one kilogram by one meter per second squared.

2. What is the chemical formula for water?

  • A) H2O2
  • B) CO2
  • C) H2O
  • D) NaCl
Correct Answer: C) H2O
Water is composed of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom, giving it the chemical formula H2O. H2O2 is hydrogen peroxide, CO2 is carbon dioxide, and NaCl is sodium chloride (salt).

3. Which organelle is known as the "powerhouse of the cell"?

  • A) Nucleus
  • B) Mitochondria
  • C) Ribosome
  • D) Golgi apparatus
Correct Answer: B) Mitochondria
Mitochondria are known as the powerhouse of the cell because they produce ATP (adenosine triphosphate), which is the energy currency of the cell through cellular respiration.

4. What is the speed of light in vacuum?

  • A) 3 × 10^8 m/s
  • B) 3 × 10^6 m/s
  • C) 3 × 10^10 m/s
  • D) 3 × 10^5 m/s
Correct Answer: A) 3 × 10^8 m/s
The speed of light in vacuum is approximately 299,792,458 meters per second, which is commonly rounded to 3 × 10^8 m/s. This is a fundamental constant in physics.

5. What is the pH value of a neutral solution?

  • A) 0
  • B) 7
  • C) 14
  • D) 10
Correct Answer: B) 7
A neutral solution has a pH of 7. Solutions with pH less than 7 are acidic, and solutions with pH greater than 7 are basic (alkaline). Pure water at 25°C has a pH of 7.
